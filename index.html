My (short) programming story

In 2006 I had just started my career as Building Engineer, after being studying a Bachelor's Degree in Technical Architecture in the Polytechnics University of Valencia but seems I was in the wrong place in the wrong moment as the spanish economic crisis was about to explode destroying mainly the economic sector which I had trained for. I was able to work intermittently till 2011 but in 2012, with a dark future ahead I found the opportunity to study something new for me, the CINE5b/EQF5 for Web App Development, a two years training to learn the foundations of the web development, and it was one of the best decisions I have made in my entire life.

I had some HTML and CSS knowledge but to be honest I was totally wrong about what was going to happen the next months. During the two following years I discovered wat became a passion, learn programming changed my life since the beginning, so much so that the though "this is what I want to do the rest of my life" was crystal clear in my mind. Instead of having bad memories about the 2008 crisis, it gave me the opportunity to find my place, so since then I'm convinced that it was undoubtedly positive for me. During those two years I learned the foundations of object oriented programming (with Java), SQL data bases, Linux, the basics of deployment technics, HTML and CSS, some Javascript, and many other small pills of knowledge. The passion I felt about coding alongside the skills and the technical knowledge I had from the University allowed me to obtain a remarkable academic result, being part of the 2014 Excellence Students Awards.

Due to that results I could choose between some companies to complete my mandatory internship. I chose Encamina to start my career due to the technological stack it offered (.Net technologies blended with state of the art technologies, something perfect for me at that point) and also its location (in the same town where I lived). During my internship I learned NodeJS and developed and deployed my first app in production, a file manager written in Node. Once I finished my internship successfully the company hired me and during the next year and a half I learned C#, and the foundations of SharePoint, which allowed me to work in different projects for financial and health related companies. In February 2016, however, I received an offer to join Capgemini office in Valencia. At that point Encamina was exploring the possibility for developers to integrate workflows and code automation tools like Nintex or K2 and I was not completely comfortable with that approach. I have never liked the black-magic-boxes as base of a solution, so the proposal from Capgemini to join the development team of their internal framework sound just perfect for me. I accepted the proposal and joined the company in which I continue to this day.

During my first year and a half at Capgemini I resumed my Java skills and learned the basics of Spring Boot framework, developing several modules for the company's internal framework Devonfw, giving technical support for projects and participating as trainer on international workshops. After this stage I was requested to start working on international projects based on Java and Spring Boot, so since then (2017) I have been working for clients from France, Italy, Sweden or Netherlands having always the opportunity to learn new technologies such as Docker, Kafka or Kubernetes and starting to design the solutions, from the APIs even to some architectures.
